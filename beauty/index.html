We are often asked

  • How many sessions of endosphere therapy are needed to see visible results?

    The number of sessions may vary depending on individual characteristics and desired outcomes. Typically, a course of 12-18 procedures is recommended, performed at specific intervals.

  • Are there any contraindications for endosphere therapy?

    While endosphere therapy is considered a safe procedure, there are some contraindications, such as pregnancy, oncological diseases, bleeding disorders, cardiovascular diseases, and others. It is advisable to consult with a doctor before starting a course.

  • What is the recovery time after endosphere therapy?

    Usually, there is no need for extended recovery after endosphere therapy. Many people can resume their regular activities immediately after the session. However, there may be occasional bruising or redness that quickly subsides.

  • How often can endosphere therapy sessions be conducted?

    It is generally recommended to have endosphere therapy sessions 1-2 times per week for optimal results. However, the frequency may be adjusted based on individual needs and specialist recommendations.

  • When can the first results be expected after starting an endosphere therapy course?

    Initial results are typically noticeable after several procedures, but the exact time may vary depending on the skin’s condition and individual body characteristics. In general, visible improvements can be expected within a few weeks.

  • Are there any additional recommendations to enhance endosphere therapy results?

    To achieve the best results, it is recommended to combine endosphere therapy with a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular physical exercise, and proper skincare. This helps improve the effectiveness of the procedures and maintain results over the long term.

  • Can endosphere therapy be used to reduce swelling?

    Yes, endosphere therapy can be effective in reducing swelling by stimulating lymphatic drainage and improving blood circulation. However, it is recommended to consult with a doctor before starting a course to determine the causes of swelling and select the optimal treatment.

  • Is endosphere therapy painful?

    No, endosphere therapy typically does not cause painful sensations. The procedure is performed using a special device that gently stimulates tissues with silicone spheres, causing no discomfort or pain. In some cases, there may be a pleasant mild pulling or warmth sensation. Many clients feel completely relaxed during the procedure, and some even fall asleep.

  • What is the difference between the original endosphere and analogs?

    The original endosphere is high-quality medical equipment developed and certified for use in cosmetology and medicine. It has undergone strict clinical trials and has proven effectiveness and safety. When purchasing the original device, specialists undergo mandatory training from the manufacturer to learn how to use it safely and effectively, ensuring optimal results for clients. Additionally, original devices are usually equipped with a timer that indicates the working time and signals when it is necessary to replace the spheres for continued effective operation. This helps maintain high-quality procedures and reduces the risk of incorrect equipment use.
